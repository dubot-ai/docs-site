## Talk is cheap. <span className="brand-accent">Resolution takes action.</span>

Support AI can explain what a customer should do next. The customer still has to find the right screen, understand the interface, and complete the work themselves.

Dubot turns a support answer into an approved action inside the product. It gives CX teams a way to help customers complete real tasks while keeping the business in control of what can happen.

## Why CX teams start the conversation

CX leaders see the gap first. Answers are getting faster, but many contacts still end with the customer doing the hard part. Dubot is designed for requests where resolution requires a product action, not another paragraph.

Examples might include updating an account setting, correcting a configuration, or completing a known workflow. Each proposed use case is reviewed during pilot design. An example here does not mean that action is generally available.

## What technical evaluators need to know

Dubot is not intended to receive unrestricted access to a product. A pilot starts with a small set of named journeys and explicit boundaries.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define the allowed outcome">
    CX and product owners choose one high-value customer task.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map the product journey">
    Dubot identifies the relevant screens, states, and completion signals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the control points">
    The team agrees what Dubot may suggest, what requires confirmation, and what remains unavailable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test before exposure">
    The journey is evaluated in a controlled environment before it reaches customers.
  </Step>
</Steps>
